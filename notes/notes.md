 Theoretical Pricing Engines (The "Greeks")Firms don't just look at the last traded price; they calculate a "Fair Value" for every contract.What they use: Custom versions of Black-Scholes for European options or Cox-Ross-Rubinstein (Binomial) for American options. For complex products, they use Monte Carlo simulations.What you can incorporate: You can integrate a specialized library to calculate Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega, Rho) and Implied Volatility (IV) on the fly.Implementation Tip: Don't just show the IV; show the IV Rank or IV Percentile. This tells a user if options are expensive or cheap relative to their own history—a core metric for professional sellers.3. Volatility Surface ModelingThis is where the pros make their money. They don't treat IV as a flat number; they map out the "Volatility Smile."What they use: They build 3D models of volatility across different strike prices and expiration dates. If a specific strike "dips" below the predicted curve, they buy it.What you can incorporate: A Volatility Smile Chart. Using a package like fl_chart in Flutter, you can plot Strike Price (X-axis) vs. Implied Volatility (Y-axis).Feature Idea: A "Skew Monitor" that alerts the user when the put-side volatility is significantly higher than the call-side, indicating market fear or hedging.4. Real-Time Risk AggregationProp traders rarely look at one position in isolation.
